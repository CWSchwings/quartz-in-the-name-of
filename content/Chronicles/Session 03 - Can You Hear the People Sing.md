# Session 03 — Can You Hear the People Sing?

*There were three plans on the table. The one involving gunpowder won immediately.*

---

## The Council of the Wreck

[[Pot]] returned to the treetop hideout with the shipwrecked survivors and brought them back to the jungle camp to plan the final assault on the [[Calvatharn]] main camp. Three approaches were proposed:

1. Gather the commanders in one place and destroy them all with gunpowder.
2. Slip aboard the ship undetected while the camp slept.
3. Continue spreading word of rebellion to turn more soldiers before any violence.

The first option carried the vote without significant debate.

## The Operation

Three teams split to their tasks:

- **[[Bishop]], [[Johanna]], and [[Caspian]]** moved to conceal powder barrels around the central meeting tent before the morning commanders' meeting.
- **[[Rene]], [[Bennett]], and [[Pot]]** went to sabotage the long guns.

Roughly two-thirds of the party ran into dinosaurs along the way and arrived to the action already hurt. The plan nevertheless proceeded.

The fire arrows found the barrels. The explosion tore through the commanders' tent. What followed was chaos — rebellion among the common soldiers, confusion in the ranks. Approximately half turned on the party; the other half joined the uprising or fled. Eventually, the camp fell.

## The Ship

Flares rose from the anchored ship. They expected a coded answer from the camp. The survivors answered wrong.

The ship began to move.

[[Pot]] went into the water and reached the ship first. He found the scientist aboard and detained her: **[[Thebrin Quass]]**, an elven woman from [[Calvatharn]]. What to do with her sparked the longest argument of the voyage.

[[Rene]] wanted her dead. He offered no reasoning that satisfied the others. [[Bishop]] wanted her off the ship. Others argued a scientist might be useful. The argument circled until the majority settled on keeping her aboard, against [[Rene]]'s objection.

## The Week of Preparation

They spent seven days making the ship ready for sea. The liberated crew, former soldiers, and other survivors were organized, basic repairs were made, and a course was set for [[Zhelesyr]]. The island they left behind had no name. No one gave it one.

The ship did not yet have a name either. That would come later.
