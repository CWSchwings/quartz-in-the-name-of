# Session 02 — Ambushed in the Brush

*The island proved itself patient. It had no shortage of ways to kill people.*

---

## The Summit

The survivors pressed uphill to scout the full extent of the island. The mountain's peak was ruled out — nesting grounds for the large flying species, and the kind of place where one goes in and does not come back. From a lower vantage they mapped what they could:

- **East:** Vast sandflats, open and exposed.
- **West:** Dense jungle, poor visibility.
- **North:** More jungle, and beyond it, a ship anchored offshore. Too far to identify.

They descended and made camp in the jungle where the canopy kept the flying dinosaurs at a distance. That night, [[Pot]] and [[Rene]] went out to scavenge firewood. They stumbled into a pack of deinonychus in the dark. By the time [[Caspian]] and [[Bennett]] reached them, both were down. The others kept them alive — barely.

## The Treetop People

Dawn brought company. More shipwrecked survivors arrived at their camp — a larger group, clearly better established on the island. They had built up in the trees: a bungalow structure above the worst of the predators.

They were cautious about [[Caspian]]. The navy insignia was a problem. After some negotiation, he was allowed into their hideout, bound.

At the bungalow, the group learned the ship to the north was a [[Calvatharn]] scientific expedition. [[Calvatharn]] was described in plain terms: a technocracy that runs on slave labor, science-forward and logical to a fault. The scientists had soldiers with them, and those soldiers had slaves.

The party restocked on supplies — leathers, bows, tool kits — from the treetop group's stores.

## The Calvatharn Outpost

They pushed deeper into the jungle, became lost for a time, and killed a carnosaur they encountered on the way. Eventually they began hearing voices. [[Bishop]] was the only one who understood the language.

Following the sounds, they tracked a regiment of [[Calvatharn]] soldiers to a cave outpost. The scientists were attempting to extract eggs from a dinosaur nest without alerting the parents. A secondary concern for the party.

The primary concern was the eighty to ninety soldiers at the main camp further on, and the commanders who led them.

## A Plan Takes Shape

A plan emerged to drive a wedge between the soldiers and their officers. A single soldier would be isolated, persuaded to defect, returned to their unit to spread the word. Once enough common soldiers turned, the commanders could be eliminated without resistance.

It worked. One commander fell. Ten soldiers joined the survivors' cause. [[Pot]] returned to the treetop people to report progress. The rest pushed deeper into the outpost.

By the time it was done, approximately thirty slave-soldiers and five to ten miners had come over. Eighty to ninety soldiers and their commanders remained at the main camp.

The island was not yet won. But the numbers had shifted.
