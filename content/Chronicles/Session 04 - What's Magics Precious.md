# Session 04 — What's "Magics", Precious?

*They were fifty souls on a stolen ship, speaking a dozen languages, and heading toward a world that had strong opinions about magic. The ocean had other opinions first.*

---

## Underway

[[Caspian]] took the helm. [[Bishop]] saw to repairs. [[Johanna]] managed the kitchens. [[Pot]] held the crow's nest. [[Rene]] was, by polite general consensus, not contributing. [[Bennett]]'s role was still being worked out.

The crew of approximately fifty struggled to communicate across a tangle of languages and recent shared trauma. Tensions ran constant. Working relationships were forged slowly, out of necessity.

## First Encounter: The Gossip

The first vessel they came upon held a single stranded sailor: **Tomal Cray**, who had funded a voyage to the New World with the intention of establishing a trading outpost. His hired ship, *The Graven Deep*, had put him over the side for being insufferable company.

He was taken aboard. His most useful quality: approximately fifteen languages spoken. He was retained as a linguist.

## Second Encounter: The Tendrils

The second vessel they sighted was wrong in the way that makes experienced sailors quiet. The hull was covered in purple viney tendrils. The crew, on closer inspection, was also covered. They appeared dead, or nearly so.

[[Rene]] was emphatic. Sink the ship. Keep sailing. Do not approach. Many aboard had little context for what he was warning them about — magic was not commonly discussed in frank terms among people who had spent their lives at sea under [[Tir Zafiri]] or merchant flags. He gave no detailed explanation. He insisted, and when that failed, he gave up trying.

The party boarded.

One crew member was alive: **[[Corren Saithe]]**. He had survived the rest — the magic had infected the ship's crew, turned them on each other, and he was the last one standing. The cause was their captain, who had been experimenting with some kind of artifact. Corren was brought aboard the party's ship.

They searched for the captain and the artifact. Found them in the captain's quarters — or what remained of the captain. A floating metallic orb hung above the empty husk of a human body. The captain had kept her log as a tattoo. They took the skin to read later. 

[[Rene]] had made his warnings. Then he touched the orb.

The orb opened. Inside was a sphere of water containing something that looked like a person built out of stars. The ship devolved into violence. The star-person fought back. Eventually they chased [[Rene]] up to the deck, and when sunlight hit them, they vanished.

The party voted to sink the ship. They did.

## Third Encounter: A Naval Battle

In the distance, a [[Vallume]] naval vessel was engaged with a pirate ship. The majority of the party decided the wisest course was to keep sailing. They kept sailing.
