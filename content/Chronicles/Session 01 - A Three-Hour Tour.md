# Session 01 — A Three-Hour Tour

*The following account was reconstructed from testimony gathered after the fact. It concerns the wreck of three vessels and the unlikely assembly of survivors who would come to call themselves the [[Captains]].*

---

## The Wreck

Three ships met in open water during a violent storm: a pirate vessel, a navy frigate flying [[Tir Zafiri]] colors, and a merchant ship. The storm was argument enough for hostilities, but the crews chose otherwise, and combat was well underway when the sea itself intervened.

A creature of enormous size — something older than any sailors' ghost story — surfaced beneath all three ships and broke them apart. There was no warning and no negotiation. The ships were simply gone. Those who survived went into the water, and those who made it to shore found themselves on an island none had charted.

They had nothing. The clothes they wore. Whatever washed ashore with them.

## The Shore

Tensions between survivors ran high. Pirates and navy sailors stood on opposite sides of a stretch of beach with merchant passengers caught between them. The jungle at the tree line held its own opinion — a pack of velociraptors emerged to remind the survivors that whatever grudges they carried from the sea, the island did not care.

That shared threat accomplished what diplomacy could not. The survivors scattered the raptors together.

**[[Caspian]]**, the navy sailor, immediately declared the pirates under arrest, naming the island their jail. This was, by general consensus, an absurd statement delivered with complete sincerity. The pirates chose not to test it.

## First Days

The group pushed inland, found a river two hours in, and began establishing shelter. Food and water were located. The local herbivore dinosaurs were convinced, through patience and some effort, to tolerate the newcomers.

A large creature in the river — believed at first to be a fish — was revealed to be a plesiosaur when [[Caspian]] attempted to wrestle it. He and [[Rene]] were pulled under before the others could drive it off.

That night, [[Johanna]] had set a pit trap. One of the herbivore dinosaurs walked into it. [[Pot]] attempted to free the animal and was struck by its tail. The dinosaur was released.

## The Lookout

The following morning the group struck uphill, seeking high ground to survey the island. Along the way they encountered a small pack of dinosaurs feeding on a dead pirate. The attempt to ambush and scatter them went poorly. [[Pot]], [[Bennett]], and [[Rene]] were all brought down before the last of the frilled deathspitters finally retreated.

They looted what remained: leather armor, a dagger, a scimitar, a tinderbox, ten bullets, ten gold.

## Survivors

Six individuals from the three ships found themselves most consistently together in these early days:

- **[[Pot]]** — tabaxi monk, pirate crew, crow's nest man
- **[[Bishop]]** — bullywug artificer, pirate crew, mute
- **[[Rene]]** — human ranger, merchant passenger
- **[[Bennett]]** — human gunslinger, merchant bodyguard
- **[[Caspian]]** — human pugilist, navy sailor
- **[[Johanna]]** — halfling rogue, navy sailor, formerly a pirate
