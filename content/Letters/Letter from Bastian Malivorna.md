# Letter from Bastian Malivorna

**To:** [[Bennett]]  
**From:** [[Bastian Malivorna]]  
**Written from:** Vaelcor's Reach

---

*Addressed to Bennett.*

Dearest Bennett,

Bennett, I'm so sorry to read that you were forced into a position like that. I know that you have lived with the burden of literacy your whole life, and your new friends are only recently literate, if at all. I know you, B, and if you'd had any other choice at all, I know you would have taken it.

B, I know you trust your friends, but it seems more and more that you need to keep yourself protected from their carelessness. Things like this increased crew count — it doesn't seem like they understand the way you do what it's like to live a life of literacy surrounded by the illiterate. I certainly don't know the sea the way you do, B, so maybe I'm off-base. I agree, finding a literate crew that large seems like an impossibility.

If you do decide to strike out on your own, I know you'll be alright. With the way you inspire loyalty I'm certain a good portion of the literate crew would choose to accompany you. If you could make your way here I'm certain I could help you acquire a small ship of your own to set out with.

In the meantime, you'd of course be welcome to stay here and relax for a time in Vaelcor's Reach. For that matter, if you just need a few weeks off the sea and away from work, I'd welcome the visit. Our shipmaster has been given your ship's name and will ensure that you have a berth here should you ever make your way north. I'd be overjoyed at the chance to see you again, and to explain to your friends the errors in their thinking.

While you're here I'm afraid I can't allow you to steal me away to the sea. I shouldn't like to see the bounty my parents would put on your head if such a thing were to be the case, and I'm afraid that my pampered self is likely unsuited to the rigors of such a life. Now, if you'd ever like to leave the life of hardship behind and become a full-time courtier, please, do let me know.

For that matter, if you receive this letter when it first arrives, the Silver Tern will be resupplying before coming back to us, and Captain Vayne would be happy to see you to our shores. Even if you don't return with him, Bennett, I'd recommend speaking with him. He's an excellent sailor and swordsman and I'm sure he'd be happy to exchange a few tricks with you.

I hope your tasks in Vallume went according to your hopes, and that any legal jeopardy your crew was in evaporated as a lot of mistaken identity. I know you wouldn't be caught involved with anything untoward. I look forward to hearing all about what you learned in Vallume, and how you learned it, when we see one another again in person.

As to me, I've just rejected the hand of yet another eligible bachelor after two months of courting, and the two debutantes after him after only a few days each. He was nice enough, but after the initial phase of getting to know one another I found we had nothing sufficiently in common to keep talking about. The two women were worse, with no seeming interests of their own beyond the latest fashion at court. You know me, B, I certainly don't shy from making a fashion statement myself, but how my parents could present to me these suitors with no other interests is beyond me.

My parents, in what they describe as necessary and I decry as an attempt to punish me, are throwing a ball in a month or so to try to trap me into a new relationship. The ball I'll enjoy. The attempts of the various parties to see me locked into a relationship much less so.

Be well, B. And please write back soon.

Yours,  
Bastian Malivorna
